Full Reference

Everything you need to grow a thriving colony fast.

🎯 Ideal Conditions

✅ Good Foods

  • Fruit & veg scraps (non-citrus)
  • Coffee grounds & paper filters
  • Tea bags (remove staples)
  • Crushed eggshells (pH buffer)
  • Shredded cardboard & newspaper
  • Bread & grains (small amounts)
  • Aged manure (horse, cow, rabbit)
  • Leaves & clippings (no pesticides)

🚫 Avoid These

  • Citrus peels — acidic, drives worms away
  • Onions & garlic — toxic in quantity
  • Meat, fish, dairy — pests + rot
  • Oily / spicy foods
  • Pet waste — disease risk
  • Glossy paper or cardboard
  • Fresh grass clippings in bulk
  • Chemically treated plant matter

🚨 Escape Troubleshooting

Too Acidic
Add eggshells or garden lime. Below 6.0 is common with lots of fruit scraps.
Too Wet / Anaerobic
Add dry cardboard and fluff bedding. Check drainage holes aren't clogged.
Too Hot
Move somewhere cooler. Add frozen water bottles. Ideal: 55–77°F.
New Bin Stress
Normal for 1–2 weeks. Leave a light on overnight — worms retreat from light.
Overfeeding
Wait until food is 80% gone. Rotting food creates ammonia and H₂S gas.
Vibration
Keep away from washers and loud music. Vibrations mimic predators.

🪱 Worm Species

Red Wigglers ⭐
Eisenia fetida
Gold standard. Fast breeder, wide conditions, ideal for totes. Best for fishing and selling.
European Nightcrawlers
Eisenia hortensis
Larger, great bait. Cooler preference (50–70°F). Strong sellers for fishing bait.
African Nightcrawlers
Eudrilus eugeniae
Very fast in warm climates. Need 70–80°F. Sensitive to cold.
Canadian Nightcrawlers
Lumbricus terrestris
Burrowers — not ideal for totes. Need cool deep soil (40–60°F).

📈 Maximize Growth

Temperature Sweet Spot
Keep at 70–75°F. Red wigglers double in 60–90 days. Every degree below 60°F slows things noticeably.
Feed Consistently
Small frequent feedings beat large dumps. Bury in different spots each time.
Darkness Is Key
Lid on, dark location. Unstressed worms breed and eat more.
Harvest Castings
Once bin is ⅔ full of dark crumbly castings, move worms to fresh bedding. Too many castings inhibits breeding.
Protein Boost
Small amounts of cornmeal or oatmeal accelerate breeding. Use sparingly — overuse overheats the bin.
Ventilation
¼" holes in lid and upper sides. Damp burlap on surface holds moisture while letting gas escape.
